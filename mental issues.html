Common Mental Health Issues

Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD) involves persistent and excessive worry about various aspects of life. Symptoms may include:

  • Restlessness
  • Fatigue
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Irritability
  • Muscle tension
  • Sleep disturbances
Treatment for GAD often includes therapy (such as Cognitive Behavioral Therapy) and medications.

Social Phobias

Social phobias are characterized by an intense fear of social situations. Symptoms may include:

  • Intense anxiety
  • Physical symptoms (e.g., sweating, trembling)
  • Avoidance of social situations
  • Difficulty speaking in social settings
Treatment for social phobias often includes therapy, such as exposure therapy, and medications.

Specific Phobias

Specific phobias involve irrational fears of specific situations or objects. Symptoms may include:

  • Intense fear or anxiety
  • Avoidance of the feared object or situation
  • Physical symptoms (e.g., rapid heartbeat, sweating)
  • Panic attacks
Treatment for specific phobias often includes therapy, such as Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), and exposure therapy.

Panic Disorders

Panic disorders involve sudden and repeated attacks of fear. Symptoms may include:

  • Sudden and intense feelings of fear or terror
  • Heart palpitations
  • Sweating
  • Trembling
  • Shortness of breath
  • Feelings of impending doom
Treatment for panic disorders often includes therapy (such as CBT) and medications (such as antidepressants).

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD) is characterized by obsessions (unwanted, intrusive thoughts) and compulsions (repetitive behaviors). Symptoms may include:

  • Obsessive thoughts
  • Compulsive behaviors
  • Fear of contamination
  • Need for symmetry or order
  • Hoarding
Treatment for OCD often includes therapy (such as Exposure and Response Prevention) and medications.

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) can develop after exposure to a traumatic event. Symptoms may include:

  • Flashbacks
  • Nightmares
  • Severe anxiety
  • Difficulty sleeping
  • Emotional numbness
Treatment for PTSD often includes therapy (such as Cognitive Processing Therapy) and medications.
