Common Mental Health Issues

Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

Generalized Anxiety Disorder involves persistent and excessive worry about various aspects of life. If you or someone you know is experiencing GAD, seeking professional help is crucial.

Social Phobias

Social phobias are characterized by an intense fear of social situations. Counseling and exposure therapies are common approaches to address social phobias.

Specific Phobias

Specific phobias, such as agoraphobia and claustrophobia, involve irrational fears of specific situations or objects. Treatment may include cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) and gradual exposure.

Panic Disorders

Panic disorders involve sudden and repeated attacks of fear. Treatment options include therapy and medications.

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

OCD is characterized by intrusive thoughts and repetitive behaviors. Treatment often includes cognitive-behavioral therapy and medications.

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

PTSD can develop after exposure to a traumatic event. Psychotherapy and medications are common interventions for managing PTSD symptoms.
